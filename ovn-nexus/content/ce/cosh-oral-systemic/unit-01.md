## Why this unit matters

Unit 0 introduced four terminal events and named the mouth as their shared upstream neighbor. That framing is enough to start a conversation. It is not enough to defend one. A physician who asks *how* an inflamed pocket ends up implicated in a coronary, a rising HbA1c, a preterm delivery, or a plaque-laden brain deserves a mechanism, not a gesture — and a mechanism stated with its evidence tier attached, so that the strong claims and the speculative ones never get spoken in the same breath.

That is the work of this unit. We take each of the four cascades — vascular, metabolic, placental, neural — and walk it link by link, from the periodontal boundary to the terminal event, stamping every step with the tier it has actually earned. Then we tie all four together with the single mechanistic spine the framework proposes: the five-step cascade of barrier disruption, mitochondrial stress, phenotypic reprogramming, extracellular-vesicle propagation, and tissue endpoint.

The discipline is the point. As a Tier-2 clinician you are not being asked to believe more than a foundations student. You are being asked to say more, more precisely — to hold a full cascade in your head and know exactly where along it your license to speak runs out.

> "The opportunity is not to collapse diseases into one disease, but to identify shared upstream interfaces worth measuring and testing."

## The shared origin: one boundary, four destinations

Every cascade in this unit begins at the same place. Recall the geometry: an ulcerated periodontal pocket, in severe disease a bleeding wound roughly the size of the palm, pressed directly against gingival capillaries, with a gram-negative biofilm on one side and the bloodstream on the other. Three cargoes cross that boundary — whole organisms (transient bacteremia), bacterial products (lipopolysaccharide and other PAMPs), and outer membrane vesicles (OMVs), the 20–250 nm particles that *Porphyromonas gingivalis* sheds constitutively and that carry concentrated virulence cargo across barriers intact bacteria cannot breach.

What differs downstream is not the source term. It is the destination tissue and the phenotype that tissue adopts under stress. The endothelium becomes sticky and stops making nitric oxide. The adipocyte and hepatocyte stop hearing insulin. The placenta inflames. The blood-brain barrier loosens and the microglia turn. One boundary, four destinations — and, the framework proposes, one conserved intermediate connecting them, which we reach at the end of this unit.

## Cascade 1 — Vascular endothelial dysfunction

The vascular arm carries the most human data, which makes it both the most defensible and the most easily overstated. Two coupled failures define it.

**Endothelial activation.** A healthy endothelium is quiescent, anti-adhesive, and vasodilatory. Chronic exposure to circulating inflammatory signals flips it into an *activated* phenotype: it upregulates a program of surface adhesion molecules that turns a non-stick lining into flypaper for leukocytes.

| Adhesion molecule | Full name | Role in the cascade |
|-------------------|-----------|---------------------|
| **E-selectin** | Endothelial selectin | Initial rolling and tethering of leukocytes |
| **ICAM-1** | Intercellular adhesion molecule-1 | Firm adhesion of monocytes |
| **VCAM-1** | Vascular cell adhesion molecule-1 | Selective capture of monocytes, lymphocytes |

Leukocytes roll, adhere firmly, transmigrate into the intima, take up oxidized lipid, and become the foam cells that seed the plaque. Sustained low-grade inflammatory tone — the kind periodontitis is documented to produce — keeps that program idling for years.

**Nitric-oxide axis suppression.** If adhesion molecules describe how the endothelium turns sticky, the NO axis describes how it loses the ability to relax. Nitric oxide, made by endothelial NO synthase (eNOS), is the master vasodilator. Pro-inflammatory cytokines and reactive oxygen species cut NO bioavailability — lowering eNOS activity and chemically scavenging NO once made. The result is **endothelial dysfunction**, measurable at the bedside as reduced **flow-mediated dilation (FMD)**, which has been shown to improve after periodontal therapy.

> Endothelial activation is not a metaphor. It is a measurable phenotype — ICAM-1, VCAM-1, and E-selectin up, NO down — and it is the recognized first step in atherosclerosis.

The tier line falls exactly where the mechanism becomes an event. Activation, NO suppression, and the ~20–30% hs-CRP drop after therapy are **Established**. Direct OMV activation of endothelium is **Supported**. That treating the mouth *prevents a heart attack* is **Hypothesis** — never say it.

## Cascade 2 — Insulin resistance

The metabolic arm is the strongest brick in the whole edifice: the one place the association is Established in *both* directions. It is a loop, not an arrow.

**Direction one — hyperglycemia degrades the periodontium.** Sustained high glucose drives non-enzymatic glycation, producing advanced glycation end-products (AGEs). When AGEs engage their receptor (RAGE) on monocytes, macrophages, and endothelium, the inflammatory response is exaggerated and poorly resolved — more TNF-α, more IL-6, more ROS — compounded by impaired neutrophil function, microvascular compromise, and delayed healing.

**Direction two — periodontitis worsens glycemic control.** The inflamed pocket is a source term feeding **TNF-α and IL-6** into the circulation. These cytokines interfere directly with the insulin-receptor signaling cascade, promoting **insulin resistance**. The disease hyperglycemia aggravates turns around and aggravates the hyperglycemia.

```
        ┌──────────────── Hyperglycemia ────────────────┐
        │                                                │
        ▼                                                │
  AGEs / RAGE signaling                        Insulin resistance
  impaired neutrophils                         (impaired signaling)
  microvascular compromise                             ▲
  delayed healing                                      │
        │                                              │
        ▼                                              │
  Periodontitis ──► TNF-α, IL-6 into circulation ──────┘
```

The mechanism and both directions are **Established**. Periodontal therapy produces a real but **adjunct-sized** HbA1c reduction — commonly ~0.3–0.4 percentage points at three-to-four months — which is Established/Supported and must always be spoken with its effect size attached. That therapy *cures or replaces* diabetes care is **not supported** — the line you never cross, even here.

## Cascade 3 — Placental inflammation

The placental arm is where enthusiasm most needs a leash. The association between periodontitis and adverse pregnancy outcomes — preterm birth, low birth weight — is real and epidemiologically observed, but the mechanism is inflammatory-plausible rather than settled, and the intervention data are famously disappointing.

**The mechanism, as proposed.** The maternal-fetal interface is exquisitely sensitive to inflammatory tone. Two routes are hypothesized. First, **hematogenous spread**: oral organisms and their products reach the placenta through transient bacteremia — *Fusobacterium nucleatum* is the organism most often recovered from amniotic fluid and placental tissue in this literature. Second, **systemic mediator elevation**: the same circulating TNF-α, IL-6, and prostaglandins that periodontitis raises can act on the myometrium and membranes, and prostaglandin-driven contractility is a recognized trigger of preterm labor. Either route converges on the same output — a placental and intrauterine inflammatory milieu that shortens gestation.

> The preterm-birth association is genuine and worth acting on through prevention — but it is an association buttressed by a plausible mechanism, not a demonstrated causal chain you can promise to interrupt with a cleaning.

**Why this arm demands extra care.** Randomized trials of periodontal treatment *during* pregnancy have generally failed to reduce preterm birth. That null result does not erase the association — it constrains what you may claim from it. The honest reading: periodontal health is part of good prenatal care and reasonable to pursue before and during pregnancy, but scaling and root planing at 24 weeks is not a proven tocolytic. The association is **Established**; the causal mechanism is **Supported**; therapy-prevents-preterm-birth is **Hypothesis**, and the trial record actively cautions against overstating it.

## Cascade 4 — Blood-brain-barrier compromise

The neural arm rests on the least human data and carries the highest cultural charge, which is a dangerous combination. Walk it precisely.

**The barrier and its breach.** The blood-brain barrier (BBB) is a tight-junctioned endothelium that normally excludes most circulating macromolecules and pathogens. Chronic systemic inflammation is a documented BBB stressor: circulating TNF-α and IL-6 can loosen tight junctions and raise permeability. The hypothesis holds that oral-derived cargo — OMVs, gingipains, LPS — crosses a compromised BBB (or reaches the CNS by direct neural routes, trigeminal or olfactory), where it activates **microglia** from a surveillant to an inflammatory (M1-like) state.

**The observation, and its ceiling.** *P. gingivalis* DNA and gingipains have been detected in Alzheimer's-affected human brain tissue, and gingipain inhibition alters pathology in mouse models. These findings are real. Their causal meaning is not settled — detection is not causation, and a failed clinical trial of a gingipain inhibitor is a reminder of how far the observation sits from a therapy.

```
   Periodontal source term (OMVs, gingipains, LPS)
                    │
      ┌─────────────┴─────────────┐
      ▼                           ▼
  Hematogenous              Direct neural routes
  (systemic IL-6/TNF-α      (trigeminal, olfactory)
   loosen BBB junctions)
      └─────────────┬─────────────┘
                    ▼
         Microglial activation (M1-like)
                    ▼
      Neuroinflammation → AD-like pathology
                (amyloid, tau)
```

Every arrow above the microglia is **Supported** at best; the arrow from neuroinflammation to clinical Alzheimer's disease is **Hypothesis**. The cardinal rule bites hardest here:

> Do NOT claim periodontal disease is a proven causal driver of Alzheimer's disease — the causal evidence is not yet there.

## The four cascades, mapped to tier

Read this table the way the course demands: strongest claims at the top, and the license to speak shrinking as you descend each cascade.

| Cascade | Terminal event | Best-supported step | Weakest / capped step | Governing tier of the *endpoint* claim |
|---------|----------------|---------------------|-----------------------|----------------------------------------|
| **Vascular** | ASCVD | Endothelial activation, NO suppression, hs-CRP ↓ ~20–30% | Therapy prevents cardiac events | Endpoint = **Hypothesis** |
| **Metabolic** | Type 2 diabetes | Bidirectional loop; TNF-α/IL-6 insulin resistance | Magnitude/meaning of HbA1c drop | Loop = **Established**; cure = not supported |
| **Placental** | Preterm birth | Association across cohorts; inflammatory plausibility | Therapy in pregnancy prevents PTB | Endpoint = **Hypothesis** (trials null) |
| **Neural** | Alzheimer's | Systemic inflammation stresses BBB; microglial activation | Oral cargo *causes* AD | Endpoint = **Hypothesis** |

Notice the shape. Two arms (vascular, metabolic) have Established mechanism; only one (metabolic) has an Established endpoint-direction association clean enough to cite without hedging the association itself. All four share the same terminal caveat: mechanism established or plausible does not equal event prevented.

## The five-step spine that connects them

Why should four different tissues, failing in four different ways, be taught as one framework? Because the OVN model proposes a single conserved intermediate sequence sitting beneath all of them — the five-step cascade. It is the mechanistic spine the four arms hang from.

```
Step 1        Step 2           Step 3            Step 4          Step 5
Barrier   →  Mitochondrial →  Phenotypic    →  Secondary   →  Tissue
Disruption   Dysfunction      Reprogramming     EV Signals     Endpoint
```

- **Step 1 — Barrier disruption.** Gingipains cleave junctional proteins (E-cadherin, occludin); OMVs and organisms enter the circulation. *(Supported)*
- **Step 2 — Mitochondrial dysfunction.** OMV cargo impairs respiration in target cells — Drp1-driven fragmentation, ROS, membrane-potential collapse, a glycolytic (Warburg-like) shift. This is the **convergence node**: the shared intermediate every downstream disease pathway passes through. *(Supported)*
- **Step 3 — Phenotypic reprogramming.** Stressed cells adopt disease-promoting phenotypes — smooth muscle calcifies, endothelium activates, epithelium undergoes EMT-like change, microglia turn M1. Epigenetic/metabolic, not mutational — and possibly reversible. *(Supported → Hypothesis, tissue-dependent)*
- **Step 4 — Secondary EV propagation.** Reprogrammed host cells shed their own EVs with altered cargo, amplifying and relaying the signal to distant tissue never directly exposed to bacteria. *(Hypothesis)*
- **Step 5 — Tissue endpoint.** The tissue-specific terminal event: plaque, dysglycemia, placental inflammation, neurodegeneration. *(Hypothesis; conserved in mechanism, divergent in manifestation)*

> Steps 1–2 are the load-bearing, better-evidenced floor of the cascade. Steps 3–5 are where the framework becomes a working hypothesis under active testing — scientifically grounded, not yet proven, and honest about the difference.

Map the four cascades onto this spine and the unit closes on itself. Endothelial activation, insulin resistance, placental inflammation, and microglial turning are all *Step 3 reprogramming events* in different tissues, driven by a shared *Step 2* mitochondrial insult, seeded by a shared *Step 1* breach. That is the claim — and its tier is Hypothesis for exactly the reason the framework exists to teach: the spine is proposed as conserved, and conservation across tissues is not proven.

## The measurement-first logic

If four endpoints sit at Hypothesis, what is a clinician licensed to *do*? The framework answers with a sequence, not a slogan:

> Prove an upstream signal, show that intervention moves it, and only then claim disease modification.

Applied to your chair, the first two steps are already walkable for the arms with Established mechanism. You can identify periodontitis. In co-management you can document a shared readout — hs-CRP and IL-6 for the vascular and metabolic arms, HbA1c for the metabolic loop. You can treat, and re-measure at 3–6 months, expecting the signal to move. What you cannot do is skip to step three and tell the patient a heart attack, a preterm birth, or a dementia was prevented. Prove the signal, move the signal — and let the number, not a causal story you cannot yet defend, carry the claim.

## The bottom line

Four terminal events, four cascades, one boundary, one proposed spine. The vascular and metabolic arms give you Established mechanism and Established readouts you can measure and move today; the metabolic loop is the single place the endpoint association itself is clean enough to state without hedging. The placental and neural arms give you real associations wrapped around plausible mechanisms — and, crucially, intervention records (null preterm-birth trials, a failed gingipain inhibitor) that actively warn against overclaiming. Beneath all four runs the five-step cascade, load-bearing at Steps 1–2 and frankly hypothetical at Steps 3–5.

The defensible posture is the same one the whole course defends:

> Oral bacterial EVs are plausible systemic effectors and a tractable upstream biomarker interface — strong enough to justify measurement, intervention studies, and disciplined translational development now.

A Tier-2 clinician's competency is not louder claims. It is the ability to walk any of these four cascades out loud, in front of a physician, and stop speaking at the exact link where the evidence stops — every time.
