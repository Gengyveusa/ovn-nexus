# Outer Membrane Vesicle (OMV) Biology

> **Source:** ovnnexus.com homepage, /science, /education (Modules 46–47)
> **Evidence tier:** Tier 2 (Supported, Not Yet Settled) — see [[02_Evidence_Tiers]]

---

## Definition

> "OMVs are nanoscale particles (20–250 nm) constitutively shed from the outer membrane of gram-negative bacteria like *P. gingivalis* and *Fusobacterium nucleatum*. Unlike planktonic bacteria, OMVs can cross epithelial barriers, evade immune clearance, and deliver concentrated virulence cargo — including proteases, lipopolysaccharide, and nucleic acids — directly to host cells at distant sites."

---

## Physical Characteristics

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Size range** | 20–250 nm diameter |
| **Structure** | Bilayer lipid membrane (derived from bacterial outer membrane) |
| **Origin** | Constitutive shedding from gram-negative bacterial outer membrane |
| **Key source organisms** | *Porphyromonas gingivalis*, *Fusobacterium nucleatum* |
| **Production** | Continuous — does not require bacterial lysis |

---

## Molecular Cargo

### Confirmed Cargo Components

| Component | Type | Function |
|-----------|------|----------|
| **Gingipains** | Cysteine proteases (RgpA, RgpB, Kgp) | Proteolytic degradation of host proteins; immune evasion; tissue destruction |
| **LPS** (heterogeneous) | Lipopolysaccharide + lipid A variants | TLR4 activation; innate immune stimulation; endothelial activation |
| **PPAD** | Peptidylarginine deiminase | Protein citrullination; potential autoimmune trigger (rheumatoid arthritis link) |
| **Fimbriae/adhesins** | Surface proteins (FimA, Mfa1) | Host cell attachment; tissue invasion; biofilm formation |
| **Nucleic acids** | DNA and RNA fragments | Horizontal gene transfer; immune stimulation via TLR9 |

### Why Cargo Concentration Matters
OMVs carry **concentrated** virulence factors — higher per-unit payload than intact bacterial cells. This means a small number of vesicles can deliver a disproportionate biological effect at distant sites.

---

## OMVs vs. Intact Bacteria

| Property | Intact *P. gingivalis* | *P. gingivalis* OMVs |
|----------|----------------------|---------------------|
| Size | 0.5–1.5 μm | 20–250 nm |
| Barrier crossing | Limited by size | Cross epithelial & endothelial barriers |
| Immune detection | More readily detected | Evade immune clearance |
| Virulence payload | Distributed | Concentrated |
| Range | Local/perioral | Systemic — blood, brain, distant organs |
| Viability required | Yes — needs living bacteria | No — vesicles are acellular |
| Detection in blood | Rare (transient bacteremia) | Detectable (emerging assays) |

## Translocation Pathways

### 1. Hematogenous Route (Blood)
- OMVs breach periodontal epithelium → enter gingival capillaries → systemic circulation
- Facilitated by ulcerated pocket epithelium in periodontitis
- Gingipains actively degrade junctional epithelium integrity

### 2. Direct Neural Route
- Potential entry via trigeminal nerve or olfactory pathways
- *P. gingivalis* DNA detected in Alzheimer's brain tissue
- OMV-specific neural translocation under active investigation (Tier 3)

### 3. Lymphatic Route
- OMVs drain via cervical lymphatics
- May interact with immune cells in lymph nodes before reaching circulation

---

## Host Cell Interactions

### Endothelial Cells
- OMVs promote **endothelial activation** (upregulation of ICAM-1, VCAM-1, E-selectin)
- Increase **monocyte adhesion** to endothelium
- Trigger **mitochondrial fragmentation** via Drp1-dependent fission
- Reduce membrane potential and ATP production
- Generate reactive oxygen species (ROS)

### Vascular Smooth Muscle Cells
- OMVs induce **osteogenic calcification** (vascular calcification pathway)
- Phenotypic switch from contractile to synthetic/osteoblastic

### Epithelial Cells
- Induce **epithelial-mesenchymal transition (EMT)**-like programs in oral epithelium
- Disrupt barrier integrity (tight junctions, E-cadherin)

### Microglia (Brain)
- OMVs induce **inflammatory phenotype** (M1-like activation)
- Neuroinflammatory cascading
- Relevance to Alzheimer's disease pathology (Tier 2–3)

### Host Extracellular Vesicles
- *P. gingivalis* infection **alters host EV release** — changes cargo composition
- Reprogrammed host EVs propagate pathology (secondary amplification — see [[04_Five_Step_Cascade]] Step 4)

## OMV Detection & Biomarker Potential

### Current State
- OMV detection in blood/saliva is an **emerging capability** (Tier 3)
- No validated clinical assay exists yet
- Potential as **upstream biomarker** for oral-systemic disease interface

### Proposed Biomarker Strategy
1. Detect OMV-specific cargo (gingipains, Pg-LPS) in blood
2. Correlate with periodontal disease severity
3. Show that periodontal intervention reduces circulating OMV load
4. Link OMV reduction to improvement in systemic biomarkers

> "Prove an upstream signal, show that intervention moves it, and only then claim disease modification."

## Key Source Organisms

### *Porphyromonas gingivalis*
- **Primary focus** of OVN Axis research
- Keystone pathogen in chronic periodontitis
- Produces gingipains (unique cysteine proteases)
- OMVs most extensively characterized

### *Fusobacterium nucleatum*
- Bridge organism in biofilm maturation
- Increasingly linked to colorectal cancer
- OMVs carry distinct virulence profile
- Cross-references with Series 4 biofilm content ([[10_Series4_Biofilm_Architecture]])
