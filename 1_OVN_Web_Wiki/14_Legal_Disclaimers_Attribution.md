## Disclaimer Components

### 1. Professional Education Only
- All content is **educational**, not clinical guidance
- Platform does not provide medical or dental advice
- Clinicians must apply their own professional judgment
- Content is intended for **licensed professionals**, not patients or the general public

### 2. No Medical Advice
- Nothing on the platform constitutes a doctor-patient or dentist-patient relationship
- Content does not replace clinical evaluation, diagnosis, or treatment planning
- Platform is not a medical device or diagnostic tool

### 3. No Causal Claims
- The platform **explicitly does not establish** a causal relationship between periodontal disease and any systemic condition
- This is consistent with the [[02_Evidence_Tiers]] framework:
  - Tier 1: Association established, causation not
  - Tier 2: Mechanism supported, not settled
  - Tier 3: Hypothesis under test
- All content is framed within these epistemic boundaries

### 4. Evidence-Caveated Statements
- Every scientific claim is tagged to an evidence tier
- Language is calibrated to tier (see [[05_Clinical_Action_Guidelines]] language guide)
- Tier 3 claims are always presented as hypotheses, never as facts

## Ethical Framework

### Scientific Ethics
1. **Epistemic honesty:** Three-tier evidence classification prevents overclaiming
2. **Transparency:** Hypotheses are labeled as hypotheses, not disguised as established facts
3. **No fear-mongering:** Statistics frame scale of opportunity, not scare patients
4. **No commercial overreach:** "Do not claim" guidelines prevent premature monetization of unproven links

### Clinical Ethics
1. **Patient safety:** Platform explicitly does not provide treatment recommendations
2. **Professional responsibility:** Clinicians must apply their own judgment
3. **Informed consent:** Language guides help clinicians communicate appropriately with patients
4. **No off-label promotion:** Evidence tiers prevent promoting unproven interventions

### Research Ethics (Future — Biomarker Tracking)
1. **De-identification:** All contributed patient data must be de-identified
2. **Consent:** Clinicians responsible for appropriate consent before contributing data
3. **Data governance:** Platform will need formal data use agreements
4. **IRB considerations:** Aggregated data analysis may require institutional review

## Regulatory Considerations

### What OVN Nexus Is NOT
- Not a medical device (no FDA regulation)
- Not a diagnostic platform (no CLIA requirements)
- Not a clinical trial registry (no ClinicalTrials.gov obligations for education content)
- Not a prescribing or treatment recommendation system

### What It MAY Become
- If biomarker tracking evolves into a formal data collection tool → IRB/regulatory considerations
- If aggregated data is used for research publications → formal research ethics review
- If platform offers CE/CME credit → accreditation body requirements
- If patient-facing content is added → different disclosure requirements

| What You CAN Do | What You CANNOT Do |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Educate professionals about oral-systemic research | Diagnose or treat patients through the platform |
| Present evidence-tiered scientific claims | Make unqualified causal claims |
