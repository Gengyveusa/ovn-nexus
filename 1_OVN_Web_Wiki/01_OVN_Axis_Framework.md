## What Is the OVN Axis?

The **Oral-Vascular-Neural (OVN) Axis** is a proposed biological framework describing how chronic oral infection — particularly periodontitis driven by gram-negative pathogens like *Porphyromonas gingivalis* — generates systemic effects through three interconnected pathways:

1. **Oral → Vascular:** Bacterial products (especially outer membrane vesicles) enter systemic circulation, promoting endothelial activation, inflammatory signaling, and vascular remodeling
2. **Oral → Neural:** Pathogen-derived cargo reaches the CNS via hematogenous or direct neural routes (e.g., trigeminal/olfactory), contributing to neuroinflammation
3. **Oral → Immune/Systemic:** Chronic periodontal inflammation elevates circulating inflammatory markers (hsCRP, IL-6), creating a low-grade systemic inflammatory state

Even a modest, measurable, upstream risk signal — one that is **modifiable in the dental chair** — could matter clinically at population scale.

## Candidate Effector: P. gingivalis OMVs

The framework centers on **outer membrane vesicles (OMVs)** from *P. gingivalis* as the primary candidate systemic effector. See [[03_OMV_Biology]] for full biology and [[04_Five_Step_Cascade]] for the proposed mechanism.

**Why OMVs over intact bacteria:**
- Nanoscale (20–250 nm) — cross barriers bacteria cannot
- Concentrate virulence factors (gingipains, LPS, PPAD, fimbriae)
- Evade immune clearance more effectively
- Detectable in blood and distant tissues

## Key Framework Principles

### 1. Epistemic Honesty
All claims are stratified into three evidence tiers ([[02_Evidence_Tiers]]). The framework explicitly separates what is established, supported, and hypothetical.

### 2. No Premature Causal Claims
> "Do NOT claim periodontal disease is a proven causal driver of ASCVD, cancer, or Alzheimer's disease — the causal evidence is not yet there."

### 3. Clinically Actionable Now
Despite causal uncertainty, the evidence supports treating periodontitis as a systemic health issue and including periodontal status in cardiometabolic assessments. See [[05_Clinical_Action_Guidelines]].

### 4. Measurement-First Strategy
> "Prove an upstream signal, show that intervention moves it, and only then claim disease modification. That path is scientifically stronger and commercially more credible."

---

## Best Current Claim (Bottom Line)

> "Oral bacterial EVs are plausible systemic effectors and a tractable upstream biomarker interface — strong enough to justify measurement, intervention studies, and disciplined translational development now."

### Strongest Supporting Evidence
- Periodontitis-ASCVD association (meta-analyses)
- OMV endothelial effects (preclinical)
- Mitochondrial stress mechanisms
- Selected neural and cancer-related model systems

### Critical Research Gaps
- Human causality demonstration
- Cargo attribution mechanisms
- Prospective biomarker validation
- Cross-tissue conservation of the proposed cascade
