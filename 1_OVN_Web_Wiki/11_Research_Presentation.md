### 2. Why the Mouth-Body Interface Matters

**Global burden statistics:**
- **32%** of global deaths — cardiovascular disease (leading cause of mortality)
- **1 in 6** deaths — cancer (pathways under investigation for oral microbial contributions)
- **~1 billion** — dementia burden projected by 2050 (neuroinflammatory drivers under study)

**Key framing:**
> "The opportunity is not to collapse diseases into one disease, but to identify shared upstream interfaces worth measuring and testing."

---

### 3. Candidate Effector: P. gingivalis OMVs

**Physical characteristics:**
- Nanometer-scale bilayer vesicles (20–250 nm diameter)
- Released constitutively from gram-negative bacteria
- Concentrate virulence factors
- Traverse tissues and body fluids more effectively than intact bacterial cells

**Molecular cargo:**
| Component | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| **Gingipains** | Proteolytic enzymes — tissue destruction, immune evasion |
| **Heterogeneous LPS** | Lipid A variants — innate immune activation |
| **PPAD** | Peptidylarginine deiminase — protein citrullination |
| **Adhesion/fimbrial components** | Host cell binding and invasion |
| **Nucleic acid molecules** | Immune stimulation, gene transfer potential |

Full OMV biology: [[03_OMV_Biology]]

### 4. Five-Step Cascade Model

| Step | Name | Evidence Level | Key Process |
|------|------|---------------|-------------|
| 1 | **Barrier Disruption** | Preclinical + Translational | OMVs increase endothelial signaling, promote monocyte adhesion, disrupt epithelial integrity |
| 2 | **Mitochondrial Dysfunction** | High-Value Mechanistic Node | Mitochondrial fragmentation (Drp1-dependent), ROS generation, ΔΨm reduction, ATP depletion |
| 3 | **Phenotypic Reprogramming** | Selected Tissues | Osteogenic calcification (VSMC), EMT (epithelium), inflammatory phenotype (microglia) |
| 4 | **Secondary EV Signals** | Emerging | Altered host EV release/cargo; pathology propagation in AD models; mitochondrial transfer in cancer |
| 5 | **Tissue-Level Endpoints** | Guardrail | Vascular calcification, oral/GI cancer progression, AD-like pathology — tissue-specific, not identical |

Full cascade detail: [[04_Five_Step_Cascade]]

### 5. Clinical Implications Justified Today

#### Actions Supported by Evidence ✅

| # | Action |
|---|--------|
| 1 | Treat periodontitis as a systemic health issue beyond local dental concerns |
| 2 | Include periodontal status in cardiometabolic and geriatric assessments |
| 3 | Expect local benefit and biomarker improvements after periodontal therapy |

#### Claims to Avoid ❌

| # | Claim to Avoid |
|---|----------------|
| 1 | Stating periodontal disease as a fully validated causal driver of ASCVD, cancer, or Alzheimer's |
| 2 | Claiming gingipain inhibition or oral therapy definitively slows dementia |
| 3 | Presenting AHA statements as resolving causality questions |

Full guidelines: [[05_Clinical_Action_Guidelines]]

### 6. Bottom Line Summary

**Best current claim:**
> "Oral bacterial EVs are plausible systemic effectors and a tractable upstream biomarker interface — strong enough to justify measurement, intervention studies, and disciplined translational development now."

**Strongest supporting evidence:**
- Periodontitis-ASCVD association
- OMV endothelial effects
- Mitochondrial stress
- Selected neural and cancer-related model systems

**Critical research gaps:**
- Human causality demonstration
- Cargo attribution mechanisms
- Prospective biomarker validation
- Evidence that the proposed cascade truly conserves across tissues

**Recommended strategy:**
> "Prove an upstream signal, show that intervention moves it, and only then claim disease modification. That path is scientifically stronger and commercially more credible."
