# Evidence Tiers — Epistemic Framework

> **Source:** ovnnexus.com homepage + /science
> **Purpose:** Classify every scientific claim on the platform into one of three evidence tiers

---

## Overview

OVN Nexus uses a three-tier epistemic hierarchy to maintain scientific rigor. Every claim on the platform — in education modules, the research presentation, and community discussions — is tagged to one of these tiers.

> "Not all science is created equal. Here is how the current evidence stacks up, presented with the same epistemic honesty you would apply to any clinical literature."

---

## Tier 1: ESTABLISHED

**Icon:** Green checkmark
**Confidence:** High — supported by meta-analyses and systematic reviews
**Label on site:** "Established Evidence"

### Claims at This Tier

| # | Claim | Evidence Base |
|---|-------|---------------|
| 1 | Periodontitis generates measurable **systemic inflammatory exposure** | Multiple meta-analyses; elevated hsCRP, IL-6, TNF-α in periodontitis patients |
| 2 | Periodontal disease associates with **atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease (ASCVD)** | Significant epidemiological association across multiple large cohort studies |
| 3 | Periodontal therapy can **improve surrogate cardiometabolic markers** | RCTs showing improvement in endothelial function and inflammatory biomarkers post-treatment |

### What "Established" Means
- Replicated across studies and populations
- Accepted by professional bodies (with caveats on causation)
- Safe to cite in clinical contexts
- Does NOT mean causation is proven — association and mechanism are established

---

## Tier 2: SUPPORTED, NOT YET SETTLED

**Icon:** Yellow/amber warning triangle
**Confidence:** Moderate — preclinical evidence strong, human data emerging
**Label on site:** "Supported, Not Yet Settled"

### Claims at This Tier

| # | Claim | Evidence Base |
|---|-------|---------------|
| 1 | *P. gingivalis* OMVs carry **concentrated virulence cargo** (gingipains, LPS, fimbriae) that can injure host cells | In vitro and animal models |
| 2 | OMVs **translocate across epithelial and endothelial barriers** | Preclinical barrier models |
| 3 | OMVs promote **endothelial activation** and local inflammatory responses | Cell culture and animal studies |
| 4 | OMVs promote **mitochondrial stress** in target cells | Endothelial cell models showing Drp1-dependent fission |
| 5 | *P. gingivalis* connects to certain **cancers** (oral/GI) | Epidemiological + mechanistic animal data |
| 6 | *P. gingivalis* connects to **neurodegenerative pathways** | Alzheimer's model systems; gingipain detection in brain tissue |

### What "Supported" Means
- Strong preclinical/mechanistic evidence
- Biologically plausible and actively studied
- Not yet confirmed in human prospective studies
- Can be discussed in educational contexts with appropriate caveats

---

## Tier 3: HYPOTHESIS UNDER ACTIVE TESTING

**Icon:** Purple/blue test tube
**Confidence:** Low-to-moderate — theoretical framework with early evidence
**Label on site:** "Hypotheses Under Active Testing"

### Claims at This Tier

| # | Claim | Status |
|---|-------|--------|
| 1 | A **conserved OMV-driven mechanism** spans vascular, tumor, and brain diseases | Theoretical — cross-tissue conservation unproven |
| 2 | Salivary/blood **OMV signals function as scalable upstream biomarkers** | Early-stage assay development |
| 3 | **Oral intervention could alter long-term systemic disease trajectories** | Requires prospective interventional trials |
| 4 | A conserved cellular reprogramming program: mitochondrial dysfunction → phenotypic plasticity → secondary EV signaling | Working hypothesis — see [[04_Five_Step_Cascade]] |

### What "Hypothesis" Means
- Scientifically grounded but unproven
- Requires prospective human data to validate
- Must be presented as hypothesis, never as established fact
- Exciting research frontier but demands disciplinary caution

---

## Tier Application Rules

### For Education Modules ([[06_Education_Series_Overview]])
- Each module clearly identifies which tier its claims fall into
- Tier 1 claims are presented as reliable clinical knowledge
- Tier 2 claims are contextualized with "preclinical evidence suggests..."
- Tier 3 claims use language like "a working hypothesis proposes..."

### For Clinical Practice ([[05_Clinical_Action_Guidelines]])
- Only Tier 1 claims justify direct clinical action
- Tier 2 claims justify awareness and monitoring
- Tier 3 claims justify research participation, not clinical claims

### For Community Discussion ([[13_Community_Research_Portal]])
- Members are expected to use tier-appropriate language
- Case discussions reference evidence tiers when making connections
